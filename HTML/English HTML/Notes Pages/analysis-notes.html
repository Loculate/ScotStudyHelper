Imagery

Video Credit to LHS English Department

The next language technique we will look at is Imagery, which can be summed up as the correlation between two things, and why the writer has compared them together. For example, in the 2023 higher paper, the writer states "plodding parade of films" to highlight the overcrowded and overproduced nature of prequels. By comparing the market of prequels to an obnoxious parade, it makes the reader associate those emotions and feelings with the prequels themselves. Imagery used in " plodding parade of films" to accentuate the uneeded, and overcrowded space of prequels. Just as a parade is obnoxious, and crammed, so too is the market for prequels saturated, and overflowing.

Tone

The language technique of tone is little more subtle, and takes more skill to pinpoint, but definitely still creates viable answers. Tone is the way an author creates a certain attitude or feeling in their writing using language techniques, for example in the paper we previously looked at, the writer creates a comedic tone in "Now bear with me, but have we, possibly, maybe — and I do not want to be judgemental — entirely run out of ideas?" to explain the absurdity of creating several identical movies. To answer this type of question, we need to first find a quote, and identify what type of attitude the writer wants to express. This could be several, such as comedic, sarcastic, critical, ironic, and many more. Then you will say how the writer achieves this, and what effect it produces.
